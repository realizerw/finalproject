Playing and Exploring

This “free-flow” style of learning enables children to play and experience things organically. This is crucial for children as it enhances their imagination, dexterity, and confidence.

Active Learning

Incorporating play based learning is a very effective way for children to develop their concentration and problem-solving skills. It allows children to think deeply and learn how to sort and use patterns naturally.

Creating and Thinking Critically

Children are naturally curious, which is why experiential learning is an essential process for developing critical thinking skills. They learn to link ideas and classify information on their own.

Play-based learning

is a naturally effective way for children to develop multiple skills and intelligence simultaneously. Studies have shown that children in enriched, play-based environments in early childhood programs had a much higher IQ at age five than those that did not. (105 vs 85 points).

Household chores

can be a great way to develop your child’s fine motor skills and boost their confidence! Watering plants, washing vegetables, or even sweeping floors can promote physical activity and teach your little ones responsibility.

Language

is the crux of communication. Getting kids to learn multiple languages from a young age can be stimulating. At Apple Tree, we offer Mandarin, Thai and English classes.
